Common Ugandan Crop Diseases
🍂

Maize Lethal Necrosis (MLN)

Crops: Maize · Symptoms: Yellowing from leaf edges, plant death · Cause: Virus complex

Prevention: Use certified MLN-tolerant seed varieties. Remove & burn infected plants immediately.

🍃

Cassava Mosaic Disease

Crops: Cassava · Symptoms: Mottled yellow-green leaves, stunting · Cause: Whitefly-spread virus

Treatment: Plant CMD-resistant varieties. Control whitefly with neem oil spray.

🔴

Tomato Blight (Early & Late)

Crops: Tomatoes · Symptoms: Dark brown spots on leaves and fruit, rotting · Cause: Fungus

Treatment: Mancozeb or Ridomil spray. Improve drainage. Avoid overhead watering.

🟤

Coffee Wilt Disease (CWD)

Crops: Coffee · Symptoms: Sudden wilting, brown streaks in stem · Cause: Fusarium fungus

Management: Remove & burn infected trees. Plant CWD-resistant Robusta clones.

⬛

Banana/Matooke Xanthomonas Wilt

Crops: Matooke, Banana · Symptoms: Yellowing leaves, internal yellowing of fruit · Cause: Bacteria

Control: Sterilise tools between plants. Remove male flower bud. Destroy affected mats.

🌾

Fall Armyworm

Crops: Maize, Sorghum, Millet · Symptoms: Holes in leaves, frass in whorl · Cause: Spodoptera frugiperda

Treatment: Emamectin benzoate spray. Push-pull intercropping with Desmodium & Napier.

🟡

Irish Potato Late Blight

Crops: Irish Potato · Symptoms: Water-soaked dark spots on leaves, white mould underside · Cause: Phytophthora

Treatment: Ridomil Gold spray every 7–10 days in wet season. Plant resistant varieties.

🐛

Bean Pod Borer

Crops: Beans · Symptoms: Holes in pods, larvae inside · Cause: Maruca vitrata moth

Treatment: Karate insecticide at pod formation. Early planting before dry season peak.

The Problem — Uganda's Farm Economy Is Broken

Uganda has 5M+ smallholder farmers contributing 24% of GDP — yet most earn less than $2/day. Here's why:

💸

Middlemen Take 60%+

Farm-gate prices are 3× lower than Kampala market prices. Brokers capture the entire value chain.

📉

Zero Price Transparency

Farmers sell without knowing market rates — often at a 40–60% discount to what buyers pay in town.

🗑️

40% Post-Harvest Waste

No cold chain, no storage, no buyers lined up = produce rots in the field after harvest.

📵

Digital Divide

70% of farmers only own basic feature phones — most "agri apps" require smartphones and data.

🚜

Last-Mile Logistics Void

No vetted transport from farm to market. Produce piles up while urban buyers run out of stock.

📚

Low-Yield Farming

80% use traditional methods. Modern techniques exist but never reach rural farmers.

💳

No Financial Access

Farmers can't get loans without crop receipts. Buyers can't pay without bank accounts.

📡

No Market Intelligence

Price data exists in silos — MAAIF reports are quarterly, not real-time, not farmer-accessible.

Frequently Asked Questions
How do I register as a farmer? ▼
Click "Join Free" in the top right corner, fill in your name, phone number and district — it takes under 2 minutes. You can also register by dialling *789# from any MTN or Airtel phone without internet.
Is AgriBridge free for farmers? ▼
Yes — listing your produce is always free. We charge a small 2.5% commission only when a transaction is completed successfully. You pay nothing until you earn.
How does the *789# USSD work? ▼
Dial *789# from any MTN or Airtel phone — even a basic Nokia or Tecno with no internet. The menu will appear on your screen. You can check prices, list produce, find buyers, and get farming tips all without data. It works in English and Luganda.
How does payment work? ▼
Buyers pay via MTN Mobile Money or Airtel Money. Funds are held securely until delivery is confirmed, then released directly to the farmer's mobile money account. No bank account needed.
Who delivers the produce? ▼
AgriBridge has a network of vetted and GPS-tracked delivery drivers. Once an order is placed, the nearest available driver is assigned. Delivery times: same-day in Kampala, 1–2 days to other districts. Full insurance on every delivery.
Is the AI Crop Doctor accurate? ▼
The AI Doctor is trained on crop disease data specific to Uganda and East Africa. It gives high-confidence diagnoses for the most common diseases affecting maize, tomatoes, cassava, beans, and coffee. For rare or uncertain cases, it recommends contacting your nearest NAADS extension officer. It is free and available 24/7.
What districts does AgriBridge cover? ▼
The marketplace accepts listings from all 135 districts of Uganda. Delivery service is currently active in Wakiso, Kampala, Mukono, Mbarara, and Jinja — expanding to all districts by end of 2026.
