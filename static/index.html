The Challenge

What Uganda's Farmers & Vendors Face

These are the real problems we set out to solve — documented through profiling over 2,700 farmers and 400+ vendors across Uganda.

📉

Price Fluctuations

Farmers lose up to 60% of value during glut seasons with no mechanism to lock prices ahead of harvest.

🔗

Too Many Middlemen

Produce passes through 4–6 hands before reaching consumers. Farmers earn only 15–25% of final price.

📵

Limited Market Access

Vendors have no reliable way to find and contact farmers for fresh, consistent produce in their area.

📦

Inconsistent Supply

Hotels and restaurants cannot plan menus because supply is unreliable, causing food waste and loss of revenue.

📚

Limited Information

Farmers in deep villages have no access to market prices, best practices or training resources.

⏰

Timing Mismatch

Produce spoils because buyers and sellers can't coordinate harvest timing and delivery schedules.

🌾

Poor Post-Harvest

Up to 40% of produce is lost post-harvest due to lack of storage knowledge, packaging and cold chain.

💳

No Credit Access

Farmers cannot access input finance because they have no credit history or digital financial profile.

🔒 Forward Contract System — How We Stabilise Prices

Farmers and buyers can lock a price 4–8 weeks before harvest. The platform holds escrow, guarantees delivery and releases payment upon quality-verified delivery. This eliminates the price shock of seasonal gluts and protects farmers' income.
